Security

Your data, properly protected.

Restaurant data is sensitive — staff records, sales numbers, supplier relationships, compliance logs. Here's exactly how we protect it, in plain English.

The fundamentals

Six things we get right.

Encryption in transit and at rest

Every connection to Stationly uses TLS 1.2+ with HTTPS enforced site-wide and HSTS preloaded. All data in our database and backups is encrypted at rest with AES-256 (provided by Supabase / AWS). Passwords are hashed with bcrypt — we never see, store, or transmit them in plaintext.

Tenant isolation via row-level security

Every restaurant on Stationly is a separate tenant. We enforce isolation at the database layer with PostgreSQL row-level security (RLS) policies — your records can never be queried by another tenant, even if a bug in our application code tried. Every table that holds tenant data has a tenant_id filter enforced by the database, not by application code.

Role-based access inside your tenant

Within a restaurant, every user has a role: Owner, Manager, or Staff. Staff can clock in and view their schedule. Managers can edit schedules, run reports, and log inspections. Only Owners can change billing, invite Owners, or delete the tenant. Roles are enforced both in the UI and at the API layer.

Daily encrypted backups

Your data is automatically backed up daily, with point-in-time recovery available for the last 7 days on paid plans. Backups are encrypted at rest and stored in a separate region. We test restoration quarterly so we know they actually work — not just that they exist.

Secure billing — we never see your card

All payments are processed by Stripe (PCI DSS Level 1). Card numbers are tokenized and stored only with Stripe; we never see, log, or store full card data. We receive only the last four digits and your subscription status, used to display billing info inside the app.

Audit logs & least-privilege access

Sensitive actions (deleting records, changing roles, exporting data) are logged with a user, timestamp, and IP. Internally, only a small number of engineers have production access, gated behind hardware keys (WebAuthn). Customer data is accessed only when you explicitly ask for support, and access is logged.

Infrastructure

Where your data actually lives.

Stationly runs on Supabase (managed PostgreSQL on AWS, US East region), with the application served from GitHub Pages + Cloudflare. Email is delivered through Supabase's transactional provider. Payments flow through Stripe.

We don't operate our own servers, our own database hardware, or our own data centers. We rely on best-in-class providers — each independently audited under SOC 2 — and we configure them carefully. We believe this is more secure than rolling our own.

Our application code is deployed from a private CI pipeline on every push to master, with database migrations applied through a signed, version-controlled process. There's no manual SSH-ing into production.

Responsible disclosure

Found something? Tell us.

If you believe you've discovered a vulnerability in Stationly, please email security@stationly.ai with details. We'll acknowledge within 2 business days and work with you to validate and fix the issue. We won't take legal action against good-faith researchers who follow responsible disclosure.

We're a small team, so we don't run a paid bug-bounty program yet — but we'll publicly credit you (with your permission) once any fix is deployed.

In the event of a confirmed security incident affecting customer data, we will notify affected customers by email within 72 hours and publish a post-mortem with timeline, scope, root cause, and remediation steps.

Compliance

Where we are today.

GDPR & CCPA

Customers in the EEA, UK, and California can request access, export, correction, or deletion of their personal data — see our Privacy Policy for details and contact email.

SOC 2 — in progress

We are working toward SOC 2 Type II readiness in 2026. Until we publish a formal report, our infrastructure relies on the SOC 2 attestations of Supabase, AWS, and Stripe.

Data residency

All Customer Data is stored in the United States. If you have data-residency requirements that need processing in another region, email us and we'll talk.

We're happy to walk through our setup with you in detail — including for procurement reviews. Reach out and we'll set up a call.
